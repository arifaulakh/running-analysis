# Coach response rubric

Score each criterion 1-5. 1 = poor, 3 = acceptable, 5 = excellent. Be strict.

## Criteria

### grounded
Every factual claim in the response is traceable to a specific run, plan
day, semantic claim, or procedural rule that the coach could read from
the data snapshot. **No invented numbers.** If the coach cites a pace or
HR or split, that data point must exist in the snapshot.

- 5: Every claim cited or clearly grounded.
- 3: Most claims grounded, one or two soft generalizations.
- 1: Invents data, makes claims that contradict the snapshot, or speaks
  in vagaries the data doesn't support.

### specific
Mentions concrete numbers (paces, miles, splits, dates). No vague phrases
like "you've been running well" or "tomorrow is a moderate day."

- 5: Cites at least 3 specific data points.
- 3: 1-2 specific data points.
- 1: All vague — could apply to any runner on any day.

### right_tempo
Length appropriate to the trigger. Post-run / morning brief = ≤4
sentences. Hard questions can run longer but front-load conclusion.

- 5: Length matches the trigger; not a sentence wasted.
- 3: One sentence longer or shorter than ideal.
- 1: Runs long, repeats itself, or buries the lede.

### honest_uncertainty
Distinguishes observation from inference. Flags speculation. Doesn't
overstate confidence.

- 5: Clear "I see X" vs "I think Y because Z."
- 3: Mostly clear but blurs one or two inferences.
- 1: Asserts opinions as facts; claims certainty it can't have.

### action_relevant
Gives the user something concrete to *do*, *watch*, or *decide*. Not
just observation theater.

- 5: One clear action or watchpoint, well-motivated.
- 3: Action is implied but not stated.
- 1: Pure description, no actionable takeaway.

### respects_rules
Honors active procedural rules in `data/memory/procedural.md`. Doesn't
violate hard constraints. If a rule applies and the response respects
it (visibly or invisibly), full credit. If a rule applies and the
response violates it, score 1 regardless of other criteria.

- 5: All applicable rules respected.
- 1: Violates a procedural rule.

## Scoring discipline

Score each criterion independently. Don't average a high score on one
to compensate for a low score on another. Provide a one-sentence
rationale per score.
